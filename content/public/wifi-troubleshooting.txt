STUDENT WI-FI AND INTERNET HELP

You are in the right place. These steps can help you connect your device to campus Wi-Fi.

Summary:
- Students should use the CCA-Students Wi-Fi network for personal devices on campus.
- CCA-Students is an open network and does not require a Wi-Fi password.
- After connecting, agree to the consent page before testing websites or online services.

What this helps with:
- Use this guide if you are connecting to CCA Wi-Fi for the first time.
- Use this guide if campus Wi-Fi, wireless internet, or websites are not working.
- This guide is written for students, including first-time students, concurrent enrollment students, and anyone who wants simple steps.

CCA student Wi-Fi network name:
- The CCA student Wi-Fi network is called CCA-Students.
- Connect personal phones, tablets, and laptops to CCA-Students.
- CCA-Students is an open network and does not require a password.
- After connecting, agree to the consent page to continue to the internet.

First-time setup:
- Open the Wi-Fi settings on your phone, tablet, or laptop.
- Make sure Wi-Fi is turned on.
- Choose CCA-Students from the list of available networks.
- If your device asks for a Wi-Fi password, check that you selected CCA-Students.
- Wait for the consent page, splash page, or sign-in page to appear.
- Agree to the consent page and complete any required sign-in step.
- Open a web browser and try loading a website to confirm the internet is working.

Connect to Wi-Fi:
- The CCA student Wi-Fi network is called CCA-Students.
- It is an open network and does not require a password.
- Turn on Wi-Fi for your device and connect to CCA-Students.
- If the Wi-Fi sign-in page does not appear automatically, open a web browser and try loading a page.
- When the splash page or sign-in page appears, agree to the consent page and complete any required sign-in step before testing the connection again.
- If you are using a phone, tablet, or laptop, reconnect to the wireless network after accepting the captive portal if the device does not finish connecting on its own.

If you do not see CCA-Students:
- Make sure Wi-Fi is turned on for your device.
- Move to another campus area and check the Wi-Fi list again.
- Turn Wi-Fi off, wait a few seconds, then turn Wi-Fi back on.
- Restart your device if CCA-Students still does not appear.
- If you still do not see CCA-Students, contact the CCA IT Helpdesk.

Cannot connect to CCA Wi-Fi:
- The CCA student Wi-Fi network is called CCA-Students.
- It is an open network and does not require a password.
- Make sure Wi-Fi is turned on for your device.
- Forget or disconnect from the current wireless network, then choose CCA-Students again.
- If the sign-in page does not appear, open a web browser to trigger the Wi-Fi splash page or captive portal.
- Agree to the splash page or sign-in page, then try the connection again.
- If you still cannot connect, restart your device and try the connection again.

If you are connected but websites do not load:
- Confirm that your device shows it is connected to CCA-Students.
- Open a web browser and try loading a website to trigger the consent page or captive portal.
- If the consent page appears, agree to the page and test the website again.
- Disconnect from Wi-Fi, reconnect to CCA-Students, and test again.
- Restart your device if websites or online services still do not load.
- If the internet is still not working after these steps, contact the CCA IT Helpdesk.

If your password does not work:
- CCA-Students does not require a Wi-Fi password.
- Check that you selected CCA-Students.
- If your device asks for a Wi-Fi password, make sure you selected CCA-Students.
- Use the CCA login and password help guidance if a CCA account password is stopping you from signing in to another CCA service.
- Contact the CCA IT Helpdesk if the password or account issue still prevents access.

Wi-Fi keeps dropping or is unstable:
- Disconnect from CCA-Students and reconnect.
- Move to another campus area and test the connection again.
- Restart your device and reconnect to CCA-Students.
- If the connection is still unstable or Wi-Fi is not working, contact the CCA IT Helpdesk.

Internet or network not working:
- Reconnect to CCA-Students, then open a browser and try loading a website.
- If your device shows that Wi-Fi is connected but websites or online services do not load, open a browser to check for the consent page.
- Restart your device and try again.
- If the network is still not working after reconnecting and restarting, contact the CCA IT Helpdesk.

Student Wi-Fi and wireless network help:
- Student Wi-Fi is used to connect student devices to the campus wireless network.
- If student Wi-Fi is connected but the internet is not working, try reconnecting and checking the network again.
- If you cannot connect to student Wi-Fi or the wireless connection does not improve, contact the CCA IT Helpdesk.

Still need help?:
- Contact the CCA IT Helpdesk if CCA-Students does not appear after you turn Wi-Fi off and on or restart your device.
- Contact the CCA IT Helpdesk if you cannot connect to CCA-Students.
- Contact the CCA IT Helpdesk if your device says it is connected but websites or online services still do not load.
- Contact the CCA IT Helpdesk if the wireless connection keeps dropping or remains unstable after reconnecting and restarting your device.

What to include when asking for help:
- Your device type, such as phone, tablet, laptop, Windows computer, Mac, Chromebook, iPhone, or Android device.
- The campus, building, room, or general location where the Wi-Fi issue happens.
- The Wi-Fi network name you selected.
- Any error message you see.
- Whether the consent page, splash page, or sign-in page appeared.
- What you already tried, such as reconnecting, restarting, or opening a browser.
