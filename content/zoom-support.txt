ZOOM SUPPORT

You are in the right place. These steps can help you join class and fix common Zoom problems.

What this helps with:
- Use this guide if you need help joining a Zoom class or fixing common Zoom problems at the Community College of Aurora.
- Zoom may be used for online, blended, or remote class meetings.
- Use the Zoom SSO steps if Zoom asks you to sign in with your CCA account.

Try this first:
1. Open the Zoom link provided by your instructor or in your course materials.
2. Follow the prompts to join the meeting.
3. Wait for the meeting window to open.
4. If the meeting does not open right away, reopen the original class link or invitation.
5. If Zoom asks to use the app or browser, choose the option that opens most reliably on your device.

Joining Zoom classes:
1. Use this section if you need help opening a Zoom class link or joining a Zoom meeting for class.
2. If you need general help with a Zoom meeting, start with this section first.
3. Open the Zoom link provided by your instructor or in your course materials.
4. Follow the prompts to join the meeting.
5. If the meeting does not open right away, reopen the link and join the Zoom meeting again.

Zoom SSO login:
1. Go to the Zoom sign-in page.
2. Choose SSO.
3. Enter the company domain CCCS-EDU.
4. Enter your S# and password.
5. Complete any verification prompt if Zoom asks for it.
6. If you are not on campus, Zoom may prompt for Duo during SSO sign-in.

Zoom company domain:
- Use CCCS-EDU as the Zoom company domain when Zoom asks for SSO.
- Enter your S# and password after choosing the CCCS-EDU domain.

Zoom license not showing:
- Wait up to 24 hours after you log in through Zoom SSO for license sync.
- If the license still does not show after the sync period, contact support for Zoom sign-in help.

Zoom not working:
1. Close Zoom completely and open it again before retrying the class link.
2. If the meeting link does not open, try joining the class from the original course link or invitation again.
3. If Zoom still is not working, try joining from your browser if that option is available.
4. If the Zoom app or browser meeting page still does not respond, restart Zoom and open the meeting link again.
5. If you are using a laptop, confirm that your internet connection is stable before joining the meeting again.

App or browser:
- Use this section if you need to decide whether to use the Zoom app or browser to join class.
- Zoom may open in the Zoom app or in a web browser, depending on your device and settings.
- If you are asked whether to use the Zoom app or browser, choose the option that opens most reliably on your device.
- If the app does not launch, look for the option to join from your browser if it is available.
- If you are using the app, make sure it is fully open before trying the class link again.

Zoom audio not working:
1. Confirm that your speakers or headphones are connected and turned on.
2. Check that Zoom is using the correct speaker output if your device has more than one audio option.
3. In Zoom, open the meeting audio controls and confirm the selected speaker is the one you want to use.
4. Allow speaker or microphone permissions in Zoom, your browser, or your device settings if audio access is blocked.
5. Leave Zoom and rejoin after confirming the audio output setting.
6. If Zoom audio still is not working, restart Zoom and try the class again.

Microphone not working:
1. Make sure your microphone is connected and not muted on the device itself.
2. Check that the meeting app is using the correct microphone in the audio settings.
3. In Zoom, open the meeting audio controls and confirm the selected microphone is the one you want to use.
4. Allow microphone permissions in Zoom, your browser, or your device settings if microphone access is blocked.
5. Leave Zoom and rejoin after confirming the microphone setting.
6. If people still cannot hear you, restart Zoom and try again.

Audio or video problems:
1. Check that your microphone, speakers, and camera are connected and turned on.
2. Make sure Zoom is using the correct microphone, speaker, and camera if your device has more than one option.
3. Allow microphone and camera permissions in your browser or app settings if Zoom asks for access.
4. Leave the meeting and rejoin after checking the device settings.
5. You can also restart the Zoom app or browser and try again.

Sign-in problems:
- If Zoom is asking you to sign in and you cannot continue, start again and use Zoom SSO with the CCCS-EDU company domain.
- Enter your S# and password when Zoom redirects you to sign in.
- If you are not on campus, Zoom may prompt for Duo during SSO sign-in.
- If the meeting link is not working, check your course instructions and try opening the link again from the original source.
- If you still cannot join because of a Zoom login, SSO, license, or Duo prompt problem, call 303-360-4900.

If that did not work:
- Check whether the problem is the class link, the Zoom app, browser access, audio, microphone, video, sign-in, or internet connection.
- Try joining again from the original class link or invitation.
- If you can join but audio or microphone still does not work, use the audio and microphone checks above.

When to contact OBL:
- Contact Online and Blended Learning (OBL) for help with course-related Zoom support, online learning tools, or questions about how Zoom is being used in a class.

Still need help with Zoom?:
- Contact the CCA IT Helpdesk for Zoom login issues, access problems, device-related trouble, or ongoing technical issues that prevent you from joining or using Zoom.
- For Zoom SSO issues or Duo assistance during Zoom sign-in, call 303-360-4900.

What to include when asking for help:
- Whether you are trying to join class, fix audio, fix your microphone, fix video, or solve a sign-in problem.
- Whether Zoom is asking for SSO, the CCCS-EDU company domain, a license, or Duo during Zoom sign-in.
- Whether you are using the Zoom app or a web browser.
- The device you are using.
- The exact error message or prompt you see.
- Whether the issue happens with one class link or more than one Zoom meeting.
- What steps you already tried.
- A screenshot if the page shows an error or warning.
