WIFI TROUBLESHOOTING

What this helps with:
- Use this guide if your wifi or Wi-Fi is not working at the Community College of Aurora.
- It covers student Wi-Fi, wireless network problems, internet connection issues, and what to do if you cannot connect.

Cannot connect to CCA Wi-Fi:
- If you cannot connect to CCA Wi-Fi, first make sure wifi is turned on for your device.
- Reconnect to the correct CCA wireless network and try again.
- If you still cannot connect, restart your device and try the connection again.

Student wifi and wireless network help:
- Student Wi-Fi is used to connect student devices to the campus wireless network.
- If student wifi is connected but the internet is not working, try reconnecting and checking the network again.
- If you cannot connect to student Wi-Fi or the wireless connection does not improve, contact CCA IT Support for help.

Wi-Fi keeps dropping or is unstable:
- If your Wi-Fi keeps dropping, disconnect and reconnect to the wireless network.
- If the internet connection is unstable, restart your device and test the connection again.
- If the connection is still unstable or wifi is not working, you may need help from CCA IT Support.

Internet or network not working:
- If the internet or network is not working, reconnect to the wireless network and test the connection again.
- If your device shows that wifi is connected but websites or online services do not load, restart your device and try again.
- If the network is still not working after reconnecting, contact CCA IT Support.

When to contact IT:
- Contact CCA IT Support if you cannot connect to wifi, the wireless network is not working, or your internet connection remains unstable after you reconnect and restart your device.
